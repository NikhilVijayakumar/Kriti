# 16. Future Scope

**Domain:** `future-scope`
**Audit Target:** The generated future-scope section.

## Standard Definition

Forward-looking research directions derived directly from gap-analysis
findings. Each item is a concrete, actionable direction — not a generic
wishlist. The gap-analysis usecase (§2.2) produces findings that become
future-scope items here, reframed as forward-looking opportunity rather
than shortcoming.

`limitations` describes weaknesses of the current work; `future-scope`
is structurally distinct — it is what the gap-analysis usecase found,
reframed as forward-looking direction.

### Expected Evidence (Deterministic)

1. **At least one future-scope item present** — a section with zero items
   is structurally empty.
2. **Items are severity-tagged** (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW or equivalent), matching
   the gap-analysis findings they derive from.
3. **No placeholder text** — no TODO, [TBD], or unfilled template markers.

### Semantic Judgment Criteria

- Are the future-scope items genuinely derived from the gap-analysis
  findings, or are they generic "future work" boilerplate unrelated to the
  paper's actual gaps?
- Is each item specific enough that a researcher could pick it up and
  pursue it, or is it too vague to be actionable?
- Does the section maintain the forward-looking framing (opportunity) rather
  than reverting to limitation framing (weakness)?
