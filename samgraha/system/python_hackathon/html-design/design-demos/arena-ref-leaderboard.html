Competition Narrative

generated across all 24 submissions

Overview

Across 24 submissions, the field separates into three loose tiers. The top four teams — led by polaris-datacore at 16.5/20 — cleared 15/20 by combining high deterministic compliance (pinned dependencies, working CI, containerized builds) with semantic scores above 75 in at least six of ten domains, indicating the underlying engineering choices were both correct and well-explained. A middle tier of eleven teams clusters between 10 and 15, typically strong in one or two domains (most often team workflow or documentation) but inconsistent elsewhere. The remaining teams fall below 10/20, generally due to missing or broken CI pipelines dragging the infrastructure and testing domains down together.

Security and AI Explanations are the two weakest domains competition-wide, with global means of 55.9 and 57.8 respectively — both more than 6 points below the next-lowest domain. This is consistent with prior hackathon cohorts: security hardening and model-decision documentation are usually the first things cut under a 48-hour deadline.

Notable Trends

Team Workflow has the tightest spread of any domain (σ = 6.9), suggesting most teams converged on similar practices — regular commits, PR-based review, issue tracking — regardless of overall skill level, likely because these are process habits rather than technical implementation choices. By contrast, Security has the widest spread (σ = 12.6): a handful of teams implemented secret scanning, dependency vulnerability checks, and least-privilege CI tokens, while most did none of it, producing a bimodal rather than normal distribution in that domain.
