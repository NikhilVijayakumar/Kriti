Competition-Wide Narrative

Summary

Seven teams cleared the full 10-domain evaluation this round, with final scores spanning 9.7 to 17.1 out of 20 — a 7.4-point spread that separates clean production-grade submissions from prototypes that still function but weren't hardened for review. neural-forge/vision-stack takes first with the only submission to clear "Success" status in more than half its domains (7 of 10 ≥ 80), driven by disciplined testing (91.4) and MLOps practice (89.5). The middle of the table — ranks 3 through 6, a span of just 4.2 points — is dominated by the Attention band: deterministic checks pass, CI is green, but semantic review consistently docks teams for missing operational detail (rollback plans, drift detection, load-tested runtime paths). voltaic-systems/api-mesh finishes last with 8 of 10 domains in Danger, reflecting an incomplete submission rather than a poorly executed one.

Key Trends

Security and MLOps carry the lowest field-wide means (63.6 and 63.5) and the widest std devs (11.9 and 14.2), making them the two domains where a strong showing separates a team from the pack fastest — a team that clears 80+ in either is virtually guaranteed a top-3 finish, and both correlate more strongly with final rank than any other domain this round. Team Workflow, by contrast, has the tightest spread (std dev 10.0) and the highest floor: even the last-place team clears 55.4, suggesting collaboration hygiene (PR review discipline, commit cadence, issue tracking) is the one dimension nearly every team got right regardless of technical outcome. Documentation remains the most bimodal domain — teams either treat it as a first-class deliverable (88.2, 76.3) or an afterthought (55.0) — with almost no middle ground, unlike the other nine domains which cluster more continuously around their means.
